Data analytics

Below is some of high impact analyses I conducted since Covid started:

  • Learning Management System analytics: During the Covid pandemic, I played a crucial role in monitoring and analyzing the online engagement of over 40,000 students at our institution. Through detailed tracking of student access, activity, video sessions, assessment completion/marks, and content behavior, I provided valuable insights that allowed management to make data-driven decisions during the uncertainty of the pandemic. My efforts resulted in the creation of 65 reports, which were used by top management to gain a better understanding of student engagement and learning outcomes. One of the reports were presented to the Minister of Higher Education by our rector.

  • Predictive analytics: Identifying students who are at risk of failing and calculating their risk profile. Using a variety of data sets, I have continuously analyzed student data to find leading indicators that allow our institution to proactively intervene before students fall behind. This has resulted in the creation of the innovative "No-Student-Left-Behind" algorithm, which is capable of accurately predicting which students are most likely to require additional support to succeed. Through rigorous cross-validation techniques, I have verified the efficacy of this algorithm, ensuring that it can effectively predict student performance and provide timely interventions to prevent academic failure.

  • A/B testing pilot: Conducted an experiment to analyze the effectiveness of nudging students to online support resources. Our group designed and implemented the pilot study, including the selection of the experimental groups and control groups, as well as the collection and analysis of data. The results provided valuable insights into which resources students respond best to, which informed the development of future interventions and support services.

  • Correlation study: Developed a framework for defining student success in higher education by conducted a correlation analysis to identify key factors that contribute to the definition of success. This study involved gathering and analyzing data from multiple sources as well as reviewing multiple definitions in literature. By identifying significant correlations, we were able to refine our definition of student success and create a more effective early warning system for at-risk students.

  • Outlier detection experiments: As the educational landscape constantly evolves, traditional rule-based approaches to nudging students may not always be effective. Through a series of outlier detection experiments, we were able to refine our credit load nudging approach by dynamically adjusting for outliers. By identifying patterns in students' operational data, we were able to create a more accurate and effective approach to nudging that accounts for unique student registration combinations.

  • Developmental modules impact analyses: I conducted a comprehensive impact analysis of developmental modules, evaluating their positive effects on the academic and non-academic experiences of students, particularly those from disadvantaged socio-economic backgrounds. This involved analyzing various data sets and identifying key performance indicators to assess the impact of the modules on student success. The findings were used to develop evidence-based recommendations to management. Each of these module annually generates more than R20 million in tuition fees for the University, making them financially beneficial. But more importantly, these modules have a direct positive impact on addressing the equity gap in our country by providing necessary support for students who come from disadvantaged backgrounds.
